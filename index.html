Our Founder

Sarauniya Hajiya TMP Chief Fanna Muhammad Kawu (Hafizahallahu Wa Ra'aha), a native of Maiduguri and current resident of Kano, is the esteemed founder of SFIDIN. A dynamic community leader, spiritual healer, and humanitarian, she established the initiative with a mission to uplift and empower marginalized Nigerians through service and sacrifice.

Descended from the noble lineage of the Prophet Muhammad (Sharifai), Hajiya Fanna holds numerous traditional and national titles, reflecting her impact across cultural, religious, and humanitarian domains. These titles include:

  • Sarauniyar Sharifan Nigeria
  • Sheikhah (Tariqa Tijjaniyya, certified by Al-Sheikh Sharif Saleh Al-Hussaini, Mufti of Nigeria and Sheikh Abdulhaad, son of Sheikh Ibrahim Nias)
  • Zinariyar Mata da Matasan Duniya
  • Sarkin Yakin Sarkin Maganin Gargajiya, Kano State
  • TCAMP, TMP
  • Chief Yeye Akinruyuwa of Erunmu Owu Kingdom
  • National Chairlady, Association of Commonwealth Traditional and Herbal Medicine Practitioners, West Africa
  • National Women Leader, Nigerian Traditional Medicine Practitioners
  • Chief Coordinator for Women & Youth Empowerment in Medicinal Cultivation, Kano State
  • Sarauniyar Magani ta Africa
  • Sarauniyar Zaman Lafiya ta Duniya

A well-respected traditional and spiritual healer, she is known for successful treatments of conditions such as AIDS, hepatitis, infertility, fibroids, and paralysis—backed by medical confirmations. She studied under the revered Sheikh Aliyu Harazimi Kano and holds dual Ijazat from Sheikh Abdul Ahad (son of Sheikh Ibrahim Inyass) and Sheikh Sharif Ibrahim Saleh Al-Husaini, Mufti of Nigeria.

Her spiritual growth has been guided by Baba Umar Tofa, and she is recognized as one of the foremost Shaykhahs and spiritual riders of her time.

Humanitarian Achievements

Hajiya Fanna has offered full scholarships for many orphans and vulnerable children, covering all stages of education including Qur’anic and western studies. She actively supports startups by offering capital and mentorship, and has helped numerous families achieve self-sufficiency.

Her compassion extends to marriage sponsorships, ensuring young women begin their matrimonial journeys with dignity. Presently, she supports over 20 orphans under her direct care, and continues to serve countless others selflessly, never asking for anything in return.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To empower underprivileged individuals and communities through sustainable development initiatives, quality education, healthcare support, and economic empowerment programs that foster self-reliance and dignity.

Our Vision

A Nigeria where every individual, regardless of background or circumstance, has access to opportunities for growth, development, and a dignified life, contributing to a more equitable and prosperous society.
